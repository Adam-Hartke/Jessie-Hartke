Crisis Response & Regulatory Operations (SVOG)

Role: Coalition Operations and Program Support Timeframe: 2020–2021 Scope: Nationwide coordination, advocacy operations, and federal relief alignment

Overview

Operated within a national coalition of independent venues during COVID-19 shutdowns, coordinating efforts to align advocacy, documentation, and operational planning under rapidly evolving federal policy conditions.

Work took place in a high-risk environment where venues faced immediate revenue loss, regulatory ambiguity, and prolonged uncertainty.

Role & Ownership

Supported coalition operations and coordination across a large, distributed network of independent venue operators.

  • Coordinated communication and operational alignment across a nationwide coalition
  • Managed information flow as federal policy, eligibility criteria, and timelines evolved
  • Aligned shared documentation, messaging, and operational narratives across stakeholders
  • Translated policy ambiguity into practical operational considerations for venue operators
  • Supported advocacy operations contributing to federal relief efforts

Execution

  • Coordinated support across a network of 3,000+ independent venues and operators
  • Organized shared documentation and talking points used in advocacy efforts
  • Tracked evolving policy language to inform planning and decision-making
  • Translated regulatory uncertainty into actionable guidance for operators
  • Maintained communication and documentation consistency across a distributed coalition
  • Sustained coordination through prolonged periods of uncertainty and shifting conditions

Outcomes

  • Contributed to coordinated advocacy efforts supporting passage of $16B+ in federal relief
  • Supported alignment across 3,000+ independent venues nationwide
  • Helped clarify sector-wide operational needs for policymakers
  • Maintained coordination and decision support across a highly distributed environment
  • Enabled more consistent understanding of evolving regulatory requirements

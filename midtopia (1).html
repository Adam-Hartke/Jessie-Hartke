Midtopia

Role: Co-Founder and Program Lead Timeframe: 2022–Present Scope: Zero-to-one program creation, operating model design, and cross-functional delivery

Context

Midtopia was created to support long-term sustainability within the independent music sector through education, programming, and partnership-driven initiatives. The organization operates in a fragmented, post-pandemic environment shaped by market consolidation and funding volatility, requiring disciplined program design, governance, and prioritization across a diverse stakeholder landscape.

The work involves building and operating programs, governance, and operating systems from zero while balancing mission-driven objectives with operational rigor, financial accountability, and realistic capacity constraints. The organization continues to operate while actively managing program evolution and long-term sustainability decisions.

My Role

I lead the design, launch, and ongoing operation of Midtopia's core programs and operating model.

Key responsibilities include:

  • Designing and managing cross-functional programs spanning education, events, partnerships, and artist support
  • Establishing governance structures, decision-making frameworks, and operating rhythms
  • Owning budgets, forecasts, and financial oversight across programs
  • Coordinating internal teams, external partners, funders, and community stakeholders
  • Translating high-level strategy into executable plans under evolving constraints

The Work

  • Designed operating models and program frameworks to support repeatable execution across initiatives
  • Built planning artifacts, timelines, and dependency mapping to coordinate parallel workstreams
  • Established budgeting, forecasting, and reporting structures to support financial discipline
  • Led cross-functional collaboration across education, programming, marketing, and operations
  • Managed program execution amid shifting funding conditions and external dependencies
  • Documented learnings and iterated program design based on outcomes, constraints, and capacity

Outcomes

  • Launched and continue to operate multiple cross-functional programs supporting artists and community partners
  • Created durable operating systems enabling repeatable execution across initiatives
  • Improved clarity and alignment across stakeholders through defined governance and planning structures
  • Maintained financial oversight while operating within real-world funding constraints
  • Produced documentation and transition artifacts supporting program continuity, evolution, and responsible long-term sustainability decisions
